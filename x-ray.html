X-Ray Imaging in Oshawa

Fast, reliable X-ray imaging for bones, joints, the chest and more, performed close to home and reported to your physician.

X-ray is a fast, widely used imaging method for examining bones, joints, the chest and other structures.

At Oshawa Advance Imaging, we provide X-ray services for patients and referring physicians across Durham Region. X-ray imaging is often the first step in assessing injuries, joint and bone concerns, and a range of other conditions indicated on your physician's requisition.

What X-Ray Imaging Can Assess

  • Bones and joints, including the arms, legs, hands and feet
  • The chest
  • The spine and skeletal structures
  • Areas of pain, injury or swelling as indicated by your physician

Common Reasons This Exam Is Requested

Your physician may request this imaging for a range of reasons, including:

  • Assessing a suspected fracture or injury
  • Investigating joint or bone pain
  • Evaluating the chest
  • Following up on a known condition
  • Other examinations indicated on your requisition

How to Prepare

Most X-ray examinations require little or no special preparation. You may be asked to remove jewellery, glasses, or clothing with metal in the area being imaged, and to wear a gown for certain exams.

If there is any chance you may be pregnant, please tell the clinic and the technologist before your exam.

What to Expect

Please bring your valid health card and your physician's requisition. For the exam, the technologist positions you so the area of interest can be clearly imaged. You may be asked to hold still or hold your breath briefly while each image is captured. The process is quick and painless.

Your Results

Your X-ray images are reviewed and interpreted by qualified professionals, and a report is sent to your referring physician. Please follow up with your physician to discuss your results and any recommended next steps.

Questions

Frequently Asked

Do I need an appointment for an X-ray?

Please call the clinic to confirm availability before visiting. We will let you know about scheduling and what to bring.

Is an X-ray safe?

X-rays use a small, carefully controlled amount of radiation to produce images. Your physician requests imaging when the diagnostic benefit outweighs the minimal exposure involved.

How long does an X-ray take?

Most X-ray examinations are quick, often taking only a few minutes once you are positioned.

Should I tell you if I might be pregnant?

Yes. If there is any chance you may be pregnant, please inform the clinic and the technologist before your X-ray.

When will my doctor get the results?

Your X-ray images are interpreted by qualified professionals and a report is sent to your referring physician to review with you.
